What “Premium” Actually Means (and what it doesn’t)

Premium isn’t automatically better for everyone. The real difference is usually feel and control, not a longer bullet list.

Premium usually improves…

  • Roller control: smoother transitions, less “robotic” rhythm.
  • Refined comfort: easier to enjoy frequently.
  • Customization: more ways to tune intensity for different users.

Premium does NOT guarantee…

  • “More pain = more results” (often the opposite for daily use).
  • Perfect fit (space + body fit still matter).
  • Better value if you won’t use it weekly.
Decision filter: if premium won’t increase your weekly use, it’s usually not worth the jump.

Canada Buying Notes (Delivery, fit, warranty)

Premium chairs are heavy and big. Most “bad purchases” happen because of space, delivery constraints, or support surprises.

Measure & clearance

  • Doorways: width matters, but also hallway turns.
  • Recline depth: confirm wall clearance at full recline.
  • Floor: protect hardwood and plan for weight.

Delivery & support

  • Placement: curbside vs in-home vs white glove.
  • Warranty: confirm Canadian coverage + parts.
  • Returns: large-item returns can be expensive.
  • Power: standard 120V outlets (no special wiring).
Quick rule: if delivery + space isn’t solved, don’t buy yet. Solve logistics first, then choose features.
